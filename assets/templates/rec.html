Compost making at home





1. In the paint bucket add holes in the bottom and in the sides to allow air to pass through.







2. Once you started adding vegetable waste it will release brown colour liquid,
if you have holes in the bottom you can collect that.
To collect that liquid you have to place a small container below the bucket as shown.



3. First we add dry leaves, if dry leaves are not available you can use cardboard
boxes or paper instead of dry leaves, next add coconut fibre. one by to add all things.



Next add all kitchen vegetable wastes the adding vegetables should be dry not watery.
Next add butter milk or curd and mix well. Butter milk helps to increase microorganism growth.





4. cover it with lid and keep this in shade, we should not keep it direct sun light.
You have placed the bucket under the staircase and its been 3 days.





5. After 3 days little bit changes a colour. Every time before adding vegetable waste mix well.
You can add vegetable waste daily or 3 days once you can add the vegetables.

6. you need to mix well every time otherwise it will start to smell bad or form insects.
If you use bigger container then it will be difficult to mix so use this kind of small container.

7. you can add all kind of vegetable waste avoid adding citrus fruit and nonveg items as it will make
your compost smell bad. Daily or 3 days once add vegetable waste and mix well till the bucket get
half full.





8.you can use tea dust, banana peel and Egg shells as it will make your compost high in nutrients.
Some vegetables like Beetroot, Guard, etc., will release more water so dry it in sunlight for 1 day and
add.

If the compost become over wet, you need to add dry leaves or wood dust or cardboard box. Before
adding crush, the leaves as small and add. You need to maintain the right moisture un the compost
then only it will help the microorganism to grow fast. If your compost is dry add very little water or
butter milk and mix well





9.After one month you will see a brown colour liquid collects in the bottom container. Collect the
liquid (200 ml) and mix it with 20 litre of water and you add it to all plants.
